What is Kefir

Kefir is a cultured, sour, acid, yogurt-like drink abounding with good bacteria and yeast, it contains more friendly probiotics than any regular yogurt.

It is made by adding kefir culture (aka grain) to milk from a cow, sheep or goat, then letting the mixture ferment for about 24 hours. Once it's strained, it's good to go.

These are not cereal grains, but grain-like colonies of yeast and lactic acid bacteria that resemble a cauliflower in appearance.

Originating from the Caucasus mountains, Kefir has been a trusted dietary staple for over 2,000 years. The word literally means ‘feel good’, and with over 40 strains of gut-friendly cultures.
